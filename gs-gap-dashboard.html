ℹ Definitions
Gold Standard (GS) — Governed: FY25 (Jul 2024–Jun 2025) locked baseline. Used for official reporting & compensation.
Gold Standard (GS) — Operating Baseline: Rolling 6/9/12-month window. For operational analysis only — not official metrics.
Nameplate Speed (Gs): Equipment manufacturer specification — static per line, SKU-independent. Represents the design ceiling; closing this gap requires CAPEX investment.
Reference Speed (Rs): MAX(Actual Speed) across ALL historical production runs on that line. Floating — updates whenever a new maximum is achieved. Not adjusted for unit size or product mix.
Actual Speed (As): Units Produced ÷ (Run Time Hours × 60). Excludes runs with speed <5 or >150 UPM, PO qty <10, line trials (7th digit = R or PO type ZNFA), China rec variants (7th digit = C), Z-variants, assembly-only scope, and Filling+Assembly on different resources.
Cycle Speed (CS): From SAP line trial data — reference only; NOT used in GS gap calculations.
GS GAP (Engineering Gap): Gs − Rs. The gap between nameplate speed and the best-ever demonstrated speed. Closing this requires CAPEX investment in equipment upgrades.
RS GAP (Operational Gap): Rs − As. The gap between best-ever demonstrated speed and current actual speed. Closing this requires operational improvements (scheduling, changeover, process stability).
GS GAP Impact $: (Quantity ÷ GS GAP) × People × Labor Cost Per Hour. RS GAP Impact $: (Quantity ÷ RS GAP) × People × Labor Cost Per Hour. People and labor cost pending Steven Johnstone sign-off.
Tier Classification: Based on As/Rs %. Gold ≥90% of Rs · Silver 75–89% · Bronze 60–74% · Other <60%. Thresholds pending Stephanie sign-off.

🤖 GS AI Assistant BETA
What is the most effective order size for Mascara?
Based on FY25 GS data, Large POs (≥2,000 units) on L67 show the lowest Labor hrs/1000u (avg 3.2 vs 5.1 for Small). OEE also improves: 71% vs 58%. Suggested: batch Mascara orders on L67 at ≥2,000 units where scheduling allows.
Which line works best with small POs for 15 ml products?
L68 shows the highest OEE for Small POs on 15 ml products (avg 64% vs 51% on L72). Speed gap to GS is also smallest on L68 (−8 UPM vs −18 UPM). Note: L72 has insufficient n for this segment (n=7, threshold is 12).
Compare speed performance for 30 ml runs on L67 vs L68
L67 averages 82 UPM (GS=95), L68 averages 78 UPM (GS=95). L67 has a smaller speed gap (−13 UPM vs −17 UPM). However, L67 shows higher scrap variability (σ=1.2 vs 0.6). For speed-critical 30 ml runs, L67 is preferred; for scrap consistency, L68 is preferred.
